# Domain 5 - Security Program Management

This domain is about managing risk, people, and process-not tools.

---

## Core Ideas You Must Understand

### Risk Decisions

What it is:  
Choosing how to handle risk: accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid.

Why it matters:  
Security always balances cost, impact, and likelihood.

How it shows up on the exam:  
Business decisions not to fix something due to cost or impact.

Common mistakes:
- Thinking all risk must be eliminated
- Confusing acceptance with negligence

---

### Policies vs Procedures

What it is:  
Policies define rules; procedures define steps.

Why it matters:  
Clarity prevents confusion during incidents.

How it shows up on the exam:  
Governance, documentation, and compliance questions.

Common mistakes:
- Reversing definitions
- Treating them as technical controls

---

### Security Awareness

What it is:  
Training users to recognize and avoid threats.

Why it matters:  
Humans are the largest attack surface.

How it shows up on the exam:  
Phishing prevention, reporting behavior.

Common mistakes:
- Expecting training to replace controls
- Assuming one-time training is enough

---

### Third-Party Risk

What it is:  
Managing risk introduced by vendors and partners.

Why it matters:  
Outsourcing doesn't outsource accountability.

How it shows up on the exam:  
Vendor due diligence, contracts, audits.

Common mistakes:
- Trusting vendors blindly
- Ignoring access vendors have

---

### Business Impact and Continuity

What it is:  
Identifying what must be restored first after disruption.

Why it matters:  
Not everything is equally critical.

How it shows up on the exam:  
BIA, recovery priorities, downtime tolerance.

Common mistakes:
- Restoring systems in the wrong order
- Treating all systems as equal

---

## Key Takeaways

- Security serves the business
- Risk is managed, not eliminated
- People and process failures cause most incidents
